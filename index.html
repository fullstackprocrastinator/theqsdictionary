The QS Dictionary

AA (Architect's Instruction)

Written instruction issued by architect under construction contracts.

Acceleration

Measures to complete works earlier than planned, often incurring additional costs.

Adjudication

Statutory dispute resolution process under Housing Grants Act 1996.

AFP (Application for Payment)

Formal request by contractor for interim payments during works.

Air Test

Assessment of building airtightness required under Part L regulations.

Architrave

Decorative moulding around door/window openings.

Backsaw

Fine-toothed saw with reinforced spine for precise cutting.

Battening

Thin timber strips used as fixing base for cladding/plasterboard.

BIM Level 2

UK government-mandated collaborative 3D modelling standard.

BoQ (Bill of Quantities)

Document itemising materials and workmanship for tendering purposes.

BREEAM

Sustainability assessment method for buildings.

Carcassing

Structural timber framework for floors/roofs.

CDM 2015

Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015.

CESSM4

Civil Engineering Standard Method of Measurement 4th Edition.

Chasing

Grooves cut into walls for concealed pipework/cabling.

CVR (Cost Value Reconciliation)

Process comparing actual costs against valuation income.

Dado Rail

Horizontal moulding protecting walls from chair backs.

Daywork

Work paid based on time spent plus materials used.

D&B (Design and Build)

Contract where contractor handles both design and construction.

Dewatering

Removal of groundwater from excavation sites.

EICR (Electrical Installation Condition Report)

Periodic inspection of fixed electrical systems.

EOT (Extension of Time)

Contractual provision for project timeline extensions.

EPC (Energy Performance Certificate)

Legally required energy efficiency rating for buildings.

FCU (Fan Coil Unit)

HVAC component for localised temperature control.

FF&E (Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment)

Non-structural elements specified in fit-out projects.

Final Account

Agreed total contract value after all adjustments.

First Fix

Initial installation of services before plastering.

Gang-Nail Plate

Metal connector for timber roof trusses.

GIFA (Gross Internal Floor Area)

Total floor area measured internally at each level.

H&S File

CDM requirement documenting building safety information.

HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning)

Mechanical systems for environmental control.

I-Joist

Engineered timber floor joist with web/flange construction.

ICE (Institution of Civil Engineers)

Professional body for civil engineers.

Jamb

Vertical side members of door/window openings.

JCT D&B 2016

Design and Build contract published by Joint Contracts Tribunal.

Key Performance Indicator (KPI)

Measurable value demonstrating effectiveness.

Knotting Solution

Sealer applied to resinous timber knots.

LADs (Liquidated Ascertained Damages)

Pre-agreed damages for late completion.

LVL (Laminated Veneer Lumber)

Engineered wood product for structural applications.

M&E Co-ordinator

Professional managing mechanical/electrical integration.

MMC (Modern Methods of Construction)

Innovative building techniques like off-site manufacturing.

NEC4

Fourth edition of New Engineering Contract suite.

Noggins

Horizontal timber braces between wall studs.

Ogee

S-shaped moulding profile used in skirting/architraves.

OS (Ordnance Survey)

Mapping service for site location plans.

PAT Testing (Portable Appliance Testing)

Electrical safety check on movable equipment.

PCSA (Pre-Construction Services Agreement)

Early contractor involvement contract.

QS (Quantity Surveyor)

Professional managing construction costs and contracts.

Quirk Bead

Moulding with recessed groove for plaster transitions.

RCD (Residual Current Device)

Life-saving electrical trip switch.

RICS (Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors)

Global professional body for surveyors.

Sarking

Under-roof layer (board or membrane) for weather protection.

SMM7

Former Standard Method of Measurement (superseded by NRM).

Tanalised Timber

Pressure-treated wood for external use.

Tender

Formal offer to carry out works at stated prices.

U-value

Measure of thermal transmittance in building elements.

UHF (Underfloor Heating)

Hydronic or electric heating systems beneath floor finishes.

VOC (Volatile Organic Compounds)

Regulated chemicals in paints/adhesives.

Valuation

Periodic assessment of work completed for payment purposes.

Wainscoting

Decorative wood panelling on lower wall sections.

WRAP (Waste & Resources Action Programme)

Organisation promoting resource efficiency.

Xylene

Solvent used in paint and coating applications.

Yield Strength

Stress at which material begins to deform plastically.

Zoning

Division of building spaces for different uses.
